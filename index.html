Executive Summary

This audit revealed 135 electrical safety deficiencies, posing significant fire and shock hazards across various facility areas. 64 findings were classified as high risk, 41 as medium, and 27 as low. A concerning trend emerged regarding overloaded circuits, particularly in the control center and LDB panels, where undersized wiring (2.5 sq mm) is protected by oversized circuit breakers (C63A MCB). Further critical risks include compromised cable insulation in the workshop, improper cable routing in the canteen, and the use of an aluminum ladder for electrical work. Recurring issues highlight a systemic lack of adherence to safe electrical practices. Several of the identified high-risk findings have the potential to cause immediate fire or shock incidents. The prevalent use of oversized circuit breakers allows for excessive current to flow through undersized wiring, creating a significant fire risk due to overheating. Damaged insulation, compromised cable glands, and improper cable routing further exacerbate these hazards. The repeated nature of these findings in critical areas like the control center suggests a lack of proper design and maintenance. Immediate corrective action is crucial to mitigate these imminent dangers. Addressing these hazards requires a multi-pronged approach. A thorough review of electrical system design and load calculations is necessary, along with immediate replacement of undersized wiring and appropriately sized circuit breakers. A comprehensive inspection and remediation of all identified cable damage and routing issues should be prioritized. Finally, enhanced electrical safety training for all personnel, including proper ladder usage and lockout/tagout procedures, is essential. * **Immediate Action:** Replace all undersized wiring and install correctly sized circuit breakers in the control center and other affected areas. * **Short-Term Action:** Inspect and repair all damaged insulation, cable routing issues, and compromised cable glands. Implement a robust lockout/tagout program. * **Long-Term Action:** Conduct a comprehensive review of electrical system design and load calculations. Implement a preventative maintenance program and provide comprehensive electrical safety training to all personnel.
