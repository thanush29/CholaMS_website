Executive Summary

This audit uncovered 135 safety deficiencies across the facility, posing significant risks to personnel and operations. Of these findings, 64 were classified as high risk, 41 as medium risk, and 27 as low risk. A concerning trend emerged around fire and shock hazards, predominantly due to inadequate electrical installations. Examples include overloaded circuits with undersized wiring and improperly protected distribution boards in critical areas like the control center, workshop, and logistics areas. These issues demand immediate attention to mitigate potential fire and electrocution risks. Several high-risk findings highlight systemic problems within the electrical infrastructure. Recurring issues include the use of 2.5 sq mm cables with C63A MCBs, exceeding safe carrying capacity and risking overheating. Damaged insulation, compromised cable glands, and improper cable routing further amplify these risks. The lack of a distribution board schedule hampers effective maintenance and troubleshooting. Additionally, the use of aluminum ladders for electrical work represents a serious shock hazard. To address these critical safety gaps, we recommend immediate corrective actions: * **Prioritize and rectify all high-risk electrical deficiencies within 30 days**, focusing on overloaded circuits, damaged insulation, and exposed conductors. * **Conduct a comprehensive review and update of electrical drawings and distribution board schedules** to ensure accurate documentation and facilitate ongoing maintenance. * **Implement a robust electrical safety training program** for all personnel working with or near electrical equipment, emphasizing safe work practices and hazard recognition.
